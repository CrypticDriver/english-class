🧠 答题心法（每题必读）

口语高分的根本顺序

1️⃣ 先有观点（Point first）
听到题先想清楚 "我的立场 / 态度是什么"——先亮出一句明确的观点，不要上来就堆句子。

2️⃣ 再造句子（Sentences follow）
观点定了，再用句式 + 词组去 支撑它（举例 / 解释 / 对比）。句子是为观点服务的，不是为了用而用。

一句话：先回答 "What do I think?"，再回答 "How do I say it?"——顺序反了就变成背句子，考官一听就知道。

🤔 为什么选这两题？

老师很可能会追问"你为什么选这两个话题"——提前想好答法，自然不卡壳

I picked these two because they sit at opposite ends of the spectrum. The "wasting time" one is light and personal — it's about a small guilty pleasure, and it lets me be a bit playful and honest instead of giving a polished answer.

The AI question is the complete opposite — it's a real, current issue that actually affects my job, so it forces me to think critically and take a clear position instead of just describing something.

中文备用（万一老师用中文问）：这两个话题刚好在两个极端。浪费时间那个比较轻松、私人，讲的是一个带点小罪恶感的小爱好，可以放松地讲、比较真实；AI 那个完全相反，是一个真实、当下、跟我工作直接相关的话题，逼着我认真思考、表明立场，而不只是描述一件事。

🛋️ 话题 ① 一个人在家最爱的“浪费时间”方式

When you're home alone, what's your favorite way to "waste time"? — Everyday Life, 轻松私人话题

🎯 核心观点（选 2-3 个你最有共鸣的）

  • “浪费时间”其实是给大脑放假 — 一直高效不是可持续的，偶尔彻底“什么都不做”反而是恢复精力的方式
  • 罪恶感来自“应该更有生产力”的社会期待 — 但这种小小的放纵恰恰是压力的解压阀
  • 选的事往往毫无意义但很治愈 — 刷短视频/追剧/打游戏，重点不是内容本身，是完全不用思考的状态
  • 升华 — 罪恶感本身说明你知道自己在偷懒，但偶尔的偷懒恰恰是自我关怀的一种方式

🗣️ 实用句式

句式用法说明
If I'm being honest, my go-to way to waste time is …坦诚开场，直接点出主题
It's completely unproductive, but it makes me weirdly happy.点出“罪恶感+快乐”的矛盾感，扣题
I know I should be doing something more useful, but I just can't help it.表达罪恶感的自然说法
It's my way of switching my brain off for a while.解释背后的心理需求，升华句
I don't feel guilty about it anymore — everyone needs a bit of that.反思式收尾，展示成熟态度

📝 Part 2 完整范文

Cue card: Describe how you like to spend time alone at home when you're not being productive.

If I'm being honest, my go-to way to waste time when I'm home alone is scrolling through short videos — the kind where you tell yourself "just five more minutes" and somehow an hour disappears.

It's completely unproductive. I'm not learning anything, I'm not exercising, I'm definitely not doing chores. But there's something about just lying on the couch, half-watching random clips, that makes me weirdly happy. It's like my brain finally gets permission to switch off for a bit.

I do feel a little guilty while I'm doing it — I know I should be doing something more useful with my time, like reading or catching up on work. There's always this small voice in my head going, "you're wasting your evening." But honestly, that little bit of guilt is almost part of the fun. It feels like a tiny act of rebellion against my own to-do list.

What I've come to realize is that I don't need to justify it every time. After a long week of being "on" for work and everyone else, having thirty minutes where I'm not trying to be productive at all is actually necessary, not lazy. So these days, I don't feel guilty about it anymore — I think everyone needs a bit of that.

🤖 话题 ② AI 会不会抢走我们的工作

Some say "AI will take our jobs." Others say "AI is just a tool." Which side do you agree with? Are you afraid? — Society, Part 3 思辨型话题

🎯 核心观点（选 2-3 个你最有共鸣的）

  • 两边都对一半 — AI 确实会取代具体任务（尤其重复性的），但"工作"是任务的集合，不是所有任务都被取代，工作会被重新定义而不是消失
  • 真正会被取代的是任务不是人 — 历史上每次技术革命（工业革命/互联网）都淘汰旧任务、创造新岗位，AI 大概率也是这个模式，只是速度更快
  • 我个人不算怕，但会保持警觉 — 我的工作里有一半是"把技术翻译成客户听得懂的话"，这部分很难被替代，但纯执行型的部分确实有风险，所以要主动往"判断力+沟通"这类AI暂时做不好的方向靠
  • 焦虑本身是有用的信号 — 完全不怕是不现实的，但焦虑应该转化成"我要学什么新技能"而不是瘫痪式恐惧

🗣️ 实用句式

句式用法说明
Honestly, I think both sides have a point, but the truth is somewhere in between.思辨型开场，避免绝对站队
AI is more likely to replace specific tasks than entire jobs.核心论点：任务 vs 工作的区分
Every major technology shift has wiped out some jobs while creating new ones we couldn't have predicted.历史类比，增加说服力
Am I worried? A little, but not paralyzed by it.坦诚回答"怕不怕"，避免过于绝对
What I try to do is focus on the parts of my job that are hard to automate, like judgment and communication.把焦虑转化为具体行动，展示成熟度

📝 Part 2 完整范文

Cue card: Describe your opinion on whether AI will replace human jobs, and explain why.

Honestly, I think both sides have a point, but the truth is somewhere in between. AI clearly isn't "just a tool" anymore — it's already doing things that used to require a whole team of people, like writing reports or handling basic customer questions. So pretending it's harmless would be naive.

But at the same time, I don't think AI is going to wipe out entire professions overnight. What I've noticed, especially in my own job, is that AI is more likely to replace specific tasks than entire jobs. For example, in my work as an account manager, AI can now pull data and draft a usage report in seconds — something that used to take me an hour. But it still can't sit with a frustrated client and figure out, from tone and context, what they're really worried about.

If I look at history, every major technology shift — the industrial revolution, the internet — has wiped out some jobs while creating new ones we couldn't have predicted at the time. I don't see why AI would be fundamentally different, just faster.

So, am I worried? A little, but not paralyzed by it. I do think about it sometimes, especially when I see how fast the tools are improving. But instead of just being anxious, what I try to do is focus on the parts of my job that are hard to automate — judgment calls, building trust with clients, explaining complicated things in a human way. Those feel like the skills worth investing in right now.

🎓 Part 3 追问 + 参考答案

考官追问参考回答（含高分表达）
What kinds of jobs are most at risk from AI?Jobs that are highly repetitive and rule-based — data entry, basic translation, simple customer support — are the most exposed, because they don't require much judgment.
Should governments do anything to prepare for AI's impact on employment?Definitely. I think retraining programs and a stronger safety net are essential, because the transition will happen faster than people can naturally adapt on their own.
Do you think AI will make people lazier or more creative?I'd say it could go either way depending on how it's used — if people offload the boring parts to AI, it can actually free them up to focus on more creative work.

💡 为什么能拿高分

  • 不站绝对队 — "both sides have a point"体现思辨深度，比简单选一边说服力更强
  • 结合自己真实工作举例 — 用TAM/客户经理的具体场景（写报告 vs 安抚客户）让抽象话题落地，考官容易记住
  • 坦诚但不失控地回答"怕不怕" — "a little, but not paralyzed"这种分寸感比"完全不怕"或"很怕"都更真实、更高级
  • 历史类比增强说服力 — 用工业革命/互联网做参照，展示宏观视角
