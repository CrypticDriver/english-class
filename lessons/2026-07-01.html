🧠 答题心法（每题必读）

口语高分的根本顺序

1️⃣ 先有观点（Point first）
听到题先想清楚 “我的立场 / 态度是什么”——支持哪边？觉得好还是坏？先亮出一句明确的观点，不要上来就堆句子。

2️⃣ 再造句子（Sentences follow）
观点定了，再用句式 + 词组去 支撑它（举例 / 解释 / 对比）。句子是为观点服务的，不是为了用而用。

一句话：先回答 “What do I think?”，再回答 “How do I say it?”——顺序反了就变成背句子，考官一听就知道。

📱 话题 ① 如果设备会说话

Describe what your devices would say about you if they could talk. — 适合 Part 2，展示个性与幽默

🎯 核心观点（选 2-3 个你最有共鸣的）

  • 手机吐槽你"机不离手" — 每天盯它 6+ 小时，一起床就看，睡前最后一眼也是它
  • 手机也吐槽你念旧 — 手机壳/桌面/设置从来不换，恋旧到有点固执
  • 冰箱嫌你乱买 — 买了菜不做、半夜偷吃、过期食物一堆
  • 手表替你担心健康 — 提醒你站起来却被忽略，熝夜还不睡
  • 反转视角（升华用） — 它们其实最懂你的秘密（搜索记录、深夜零食、没人看见的孤独）

🗣️ 实用句式

句式用法说明
If my phone could talk, it would probably complain that I never put it down.complain that… 拟人化开场万能句
My fridge would tease me for buying vegetables I never actually cook.tease sb. for… “因…而取笑”
If my phone could talk, it would say I hold onto the past — I never even change the wallpaper.hold onto the past = 念旧/不放手过去，讲习惯很贴切
My watch would nag me about not getting enough sleep.nag sb. about… 唱叨的固定搭配
These devices know me better than most of my friends do.升华句核心结构
It's a bit scary how much my phone has on me — my searches, my secrets.have sth. on sb. = 揌住某人把柄，地道
I'd like to think my watch would be proud of my steps, but who am I kidding.who am I kidding 自嘲，超自然口语

📝 Part 2 完整范文

If my devices could suddenly talk, I think I'd be in a lot of trouble — honestly, they'd probably gang up on me.

Let's start with my phone, because it's the one I'm closest to — maybe too close. It would definitely call me out for being glued to the screen. I check it the moment I wake up, and it's the last thing I see before bed. It would probably say something like, "Do you even remember life before me?" And the embarrassing part is, it knows all my secrets — my random 2 a.m. searches, the videos I'd never admit to watching.

Then there's my smartwatch, which would basically be the health-obsessed friend in the group. It's always nagging me about standing up, drinking water, getting more sleep. If it could talk, it would sigh and say, "I told you to go to bed at eleven, and here you are scrolling at one."

And finally, my fridge — that one would just roast me. It would tease me for buying fresh vegetables with good intentions and then letting them go bad while I order takeout instead.

But joking aside, I think there's something a little unsettling about it. These devices know me better than some of my closest friends do. They see the version of me that nobody else does — the late-night snacker, the procrastinator, the person who talks big but doesn't always follow through.

So if they could talk, I think they'd have a lot to say — and most of it would be painfully accurate.

💡 为什么能拿高分

  • 拟人化贯穿全篇 — 三个设备三种性格（损友 / 健康狂 / 毒舌厨房），有画面感
  • 幽默 + 深度 — 前半搞笑，joking aside 一转进入"设备比朋友更懂我"的反思
  • 自然口语标记 — honestly / the embarrassing part is / who am I kidding 让你像母语者，不是背书

🤝 话题 ② 更连接还是更孤独

Are people more connected or more lonely than 10 years ago? — 偏 Part 3 思辨，句式更"论证型"

🎯 核心观点（两面都谈，再选一边）

➕ "更连接"这一面
  • 随时能联系到任何人，跨国视频通话像面对面
  • 找到同好社群更容易（小众爱好也有圈子）
➖ "更孤独"这一面（通常更有说服力）
  • "connected but alone" — 点赞代替真实陪伴，群里 500 人却没人能深夜倾诉
  • 面对面社交能力退化，宁可发消息也不愿打电话
  • 社交媒体制造"别人都过得更好"的焦虑感

🗣️ 实用句式

句式用法说明
On the surface, we're more connected than ever, but deep down, many people feel lonelier.经典"表面 vs 深层"对比开场
We have hundreds of online friends, yet few people we can truly count on.count on = 依靠，反差有力
Social media gives us the illusion of connection.illusion of… 高分抽象名词结构
Ten years ago, people actually called each other; now we just send texts.今昔对比，actually 加强语气
Everyone online seems to be living their best life.地道俚语，讲社媒焦虑必备
Technology has made us physically connected but emotionally distant.对仗金句，直接定调

📝 Part 2 完整范文

Cue card: Describe how technology has changed the way people connect with each other.

I think this is one of those questions where the honest answer is "it's complicated." On the surface, we're more connected than ever — but I'd argue that, deep down, a lot of people actually feel lonelier.

Let me start with the good side. Ten years ago, staying in touch with someone abroad was a real effort. Now I can video-call my friends on the other side of the world, and it almost feels like we're in the same room. On top of that, the internet makes it so easy to find your people — whatever niche hobby you're into, there's a community out there.

But here's the thing that bugs me. Even though we have hundreds of so-called friends online, most of us have very few people we can truly count on. We've kind of traded quality for quantity. A "like" or a quick comment has replaced actually sitting down and talking.

And social media makes it worse, because everyone online seems to be living their best life. You end up comparing your ordinary day to other people's highlights, and that quietly chips away at how you feel.

So if I had to pick a side, I'd say we're physically connected but emotionally distant. The technology is amazing, but it gives us the illusion of connection more than the real thing.

🎓 Part 3 追问 + 参考答案

考官追问参考回答（含高分表达）
Do you think social media brings people closer?In some ways yes, but it's a double-edged sword. It keeps us in touch, but it also replaces deep conversations with shallow ones. I'd say it widens our network but weakens our bonds.
Why do people feel lonely despite being connected?Because online interaction is often surface-level. Real intimacy comes from being vulnerable, and you can't really do that through likes and emojis. There's a big gap between being seen and being known.
How can people build real connections today?I think it comes down to being intentional — putting the phone down, meeting face to face, and having conversations without a screen in between. Quality time matters more than constant contact.
